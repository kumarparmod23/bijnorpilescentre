Chronic constipation is the single biggest reason behind almost every case of piles, fissure and fistula. Fix the constipation, and most anorectal problems either heal faster or never return.

## Why Constipation Causes Piles & Fissure
Hard stools force you to strain. Straining puts huge pressure on the anal blood vessels (causing piles) and tears the lining (causing fissure). Repeated infection from this damage can lead to fistula.

## Common Causes of Constipation
- Low-fibre diet
- Inadequate water intake
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Ignoring the urge to go
- Excess refined and processed food
- Stress and irregular routine
- Certain medications

## How to Fix It Naturally
1. **Drink 3 litres of water daily**
2. **Eat 25–30g of fibre** — fruits, vegetables, whole grains
3. **Walk 30 minutes daily**
4. **Fixed bathroom time** — train your gut
5. **Never delay the urge**
6. **Isabgol** — 1 teaspoon in warm water at night
7. **Sleep 7–8 hours** — gut health depends on rest

## Warning Signs — See a Doctor
- Constipation lasting more than 3 weeks
- Blood in stool
- Severe pain
- Sudden weight loss
- Family history of colon cancer

## Squatting Position Helps
Indian-style toilets actually align the rectum better than Western toilets. If you use a Western toilet, place a small footstool under your feet — it reduces straining dramatically.

## FAQs
### Q1. Are laxatives safe long-term?
No — they should only be used under medical guidance.
### Q2. Can probiotics help?
Yes — daily curd, buttermilk or probiotic capsules improve gut motility.
### Q3. Is constipation hereditary?
Lifestyle, not genes, is usually to blame.

## Conclusion
Most anorectal misery starts in the digestive tract. Heal your gut first — and the rest follows.
