Piles — also called hemorrhoids or *bawasir* — are swollen blood vessels in and around the anus and lower rectum. They are extremely common, affecting nearly 1 in 2 adults at some point in life. The good news is that today's treatments are painless, scientific and highly effective.

## What Exactly Are Piles?
Piles form when the cushions of veins inside the anal canal become enlarged. They can be **internal** (inside the rectum) or **external** (under the skin around the anus).

## Common Causes
- Long-standing constipation and straining
- Low-fibre diet
- Sitting for hours (office jobs)
- Pregnancy
- Obesity and lack of exercise

## Symptoms to Watch For
- Bright red bleeding while passing stool
- A lump or swelling near the anus
- Itching, burning or pain
- A feeling of incomplete evacuation

If you notice any of these, please consult a qualified proctologist promptly.

## Diet & Lifestyle Tips
- Drink 3 litres of water daily
- Eat fibre-rich foods (fruits, vegetables, whole grains)
- Avoid spicy and oily food
- Walk 30 minutes a day
- Never delay or strain during bowel movements

## When to See a Doctor
See a doctor immediately if you notice persistent bleeding, severe pain, or a lump that does not go away. Early treatment means easier and faster recovery.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1. Is piles surgery painful?
Modern procedures like Kshar Karma and Rubber Band Ligation are **virtually painless** and done as day-care.

### Q2. Will piles come back after treatment?
With correct treatment and lifestyle changes, recurrence is rare.

### Q3. Can piles heal without treatment?
Very early grade-1 piles may improve with diet, but moderate to severe piles need medical care.
