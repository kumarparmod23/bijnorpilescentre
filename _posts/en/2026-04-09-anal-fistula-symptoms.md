An anal fistula is an abnormal small tunnel that develops between the inside of the anus and the skin around it. It usually starts as an abscess and, if untreated, drains pus or stool, causing pain and embarrassment.

## What is a Fistula?
A fistula has two openings — one inside the anal canal and one on the skin near the anus. Pus, blood or fluid keeps draining through it.

## Warning Symptoms
- Recurring boils or abscesses near the anus
- Pus, blood or foul-smelling discharge
- Throbbing pain that worsens when sitting
- Skin irritation around the anus
- Fever in severe cases

## Why Surgery Alone Often Fails
Conventional fistula surgery (fistulotomy) has a high recurrence rate — sometimes up to 30% — and can affect continence.
