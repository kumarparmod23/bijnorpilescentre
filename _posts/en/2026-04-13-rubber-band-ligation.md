Rubber Band Ligation (RBL) is one of the most effective and least invasive treatments for internal piles. It takes just 10 minutes, requires no anaesthesia, and you walk out the same day.

## How It Works
A small rubber band is placed around the base of an internal pile using a special device. The band cuts off the blood supply, and within 5–7 days the pile shrinks and falls off naturally during a bowel movement.

## Why Patients Love RBL
- 10-minute procedure
- No cuts, no stitches, no anaesthesia
- Day-care — return home immediately
- Highly effective for Grade 2 & 3 piles
- Affordable

## Who is It For?
- Internal piles (Grade 2 and Grade 3)
- Bleeding piles
- Patients who don't want surgery
- Adults of any age

It is generally **not** suitable for external piles or very large prolapsed piles.

## What to Expect
- Mild fullness or discomfort for 24–48 hours
- A small amount of bleeding when the pile drops off (5–7 days later)
- Usually 2–3 sessions, spaced 4–6 weeks apart

## Aftercare Tips
- Use stool softeners for the first week
- Sitz baths twice daily
- Avoid heavy lifting and strenuous exercise for 3 days
- High-fibre diet, plenty of water

## FAQs
### Q1. Is RBL painful?
Most patients feel only mild pressure for a day.
### Q2. When can I return to work?
Same day or the next day.
### Q3. Will I bleed afterwards?
A small amount when the pile detaches — this is normal.
