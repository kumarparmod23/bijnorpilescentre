The treatment is only half the battle — what you do in the first 2 weeks after determines how fast and how well you heal. Here are the key recovery tips every piles patient should follow.

## 1. Sitz Baths — Your Best Friend
Sit in warm (not hot) water for 10 minutes, twice or thrice daily. It soothes pain, reduces swelling and prevents infection.

## 2. Stay on a Soft-Stool Diet
- Plenty of fruits, vegetables, dals
- Isabgol at night
- Avoid spicy, oily, fried food
- 3 litres of water daily

## 3. Take All Prescribed Medicines
Antibiotics, pain relievers and stool softeners — take exactly as advised.

## 4. Avoid Heavy Lifting
For at least 1 week, don't lift anything heavier than 5 kg.

## 5. Don't Strain on the Toilet
If nothing happens in 5 minutes, get up. Straining can undo the entire treatment.

## 6. Keep the Area Clean & Dry
- Gently wash with water (no harsh soap)
- Pat dry with a soft towel
- Wear loose cotton underwear

## 7. Rest, But Move a Little
Walk for 10–15 minutes a few times a day. Total bed rest is not necessary.

## 8. Avoid Long Sitting
Use a soft cushion if you must sit for work. Stand up every 30 minutes.

## 9. Avoid Alcohol & Smoking
Both slow down healing.

## 10. Attend All Follow-Up Visits
Even if you feel fine — your doctor will check healing and adjust treatment.

## Warning Signs — Call Your Doctor
- Heavy bleeding
- High fever
- Severe pain not relieved by medication
- Difficulty passing urine
- Foul discharge

## FAQs
### Q1. When can I return to work?
Most patients within 1–3 days.
### Q2. Can I exercise?
Light walking only for 1 week; gym after 2 weeks.
### Q3. When will I be fully healed?
Usually within 2–3 weeks for day-care procedures.

## Conclusion
Healing is in your hands. Follow these tips and enjoy a fast, comfortable recovery.
