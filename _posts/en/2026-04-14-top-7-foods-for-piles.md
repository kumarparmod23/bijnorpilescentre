What you eat directly affects piles. The right foods soften stool, reduce straining and ease symptoms — sometimes within days. Here are the top 7 foods every piles patient should add to their daily plate.

## 1. Papaya
Rich in fibre and natural enzymes, papaya is a gentle, effective laxative. Eat half a ripe papaya every morning.

## 2. Soaked Raisins (Munakka)
Soak 8–10 raisins overnight and eat them on an empty stomach. They soften stool and improve digestion.

## 3. Whole Grains (Oats, Wheat, Brown Rice)
Whole grains add insoluble fibre that bulks up stool and prevents constipation.

## 4. Green Leafy Vegetables
Spinach, methi, mustard greens — all rich in fibre, iron and water content. Helps prevent the bleeding-related anaemia common in piles.

## 5. Buttermilk (Chaas)
A daily glass of fresh buttermilk improves gut flora and helps digestion, easing constipation.

## 6. Isabgol (Psyllium Husk)
1 teaspoon in warm water at night creates soft, formed stools — the single best home remedy for piles.

## 7. Bananas
Ripe bananas are easy to digest, provide fibre, and help in healing of irritated tissues.

## Bonus: Drink Water!
3 litres a day. Without enough water, even fibre cannot help.
