Kshar Karma is an ancient Ayurvedic technique that uses a specially prepared alkaline (kshara) paste to chemically shrink piles. It is **painless, scar-free, and done as a 15-minute outpatient procedure**.

## What is Kshar Karma?
A medicated alkaline paste (made from Apamarga and other herbs) is applied directly to the pile mass under proctoscopic guidance. The paste cauterises the tissue, which then dries up and falls off within a few days.

## Why It Works So Well
- No cuts, no stitches
- No general anaesthesia
- No bed rest
- Same-day discharge
- Very low recurrence

## Step-by-Step Procedure
1. Brief examination & proctoscopy
2. Application of kshara paste (1–2 minutes)
3. Wash and observation for 30 minutes
4. Patient walks out — no hospitalisation

## Best Suited For
- Grade 1 and Grade 2 internal piles
- Patients afraid of surgery
- Bleeding piles without prolapse
- Elderly or high-risk patients

## After-Care Tips
- High-fibre diet
- Plenty of fluids
- Sitz baths twice daily
- Avoid heavy lifting for a week
- Take prescribed medication

## FAQs
### Q1. Will it hurt?
No — patients describe only a mild burning sensation that quickly subsides.
### Q2. How many sittings are needed?
Most patients need just 1–3 sittings.
### Q3. Will the piles come back?
With diet correction and lifestyle changes, recurrence is uncommon.
