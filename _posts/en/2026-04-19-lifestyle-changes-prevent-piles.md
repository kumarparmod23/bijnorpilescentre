The best treatment for piles is to never get them in the first place. Just a handful of daily habits can keep you piles-free for life.

## 1. Drink Enough Water
At least 2.5–3 litres daily. Dehydration is the fastest path to constipation.

## 2. Eat Fibre Every Meal
Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, sprouts and dals — aim for 25–30g of fibre per day.

## 3. Walk 30 Minutes Daily
Movement keeps the bowels moving. A brisk walk after dinner is golden.

## 4. Fix a Toilet Time
Train your body — same time every morning. The colon loves routine.

## 5. Never Strain
If nothing happens in 3–5 minutes, get up and try later. Straining damages anal veins.

## 6. Don't Sit Too Long
If your job requires long hours of sitting, stand and stretch every 45 minutes.

## 7. Maintain Healthy Weight
Obesity puts extra pressure on pelvic veins.

## 8. Limit Spicy & Oily Food
They irritate the anal lining and slow digestion.

## 9. Quit Smoking & Limit Alcohol
Both worsen circulation and digestion.

## 10. Manage Stress
Stress causes IBS, constipation and worsens flare-ups. Try yoga, meditation or breathing exercises.

## When to See a Doctor
If you notice bleeding, pain, itching or a lump — don't wait. Early consultation makes treatment easy.

## FAQs
### Q1. Can prevention reverse mild piles?
Yes — Grade 1 piles often improve dramatically.
### Q2. Is exercise safe with piles?
Mild walking and yoga, yes. Heavy weight-lifting, no.
### Q3. Do I need a special diet for life?
A balanced fibre-rich diet is good for everyone.
